1. About

A Salonnière's goal was to host a gathering for intellectual individuals, namely men, in France in the 1700s. Think of this index as a Salonnière's development of topics, with the intellectuals being community organizers and activists, whose identities vary in a multitude of ways, united by the guiding force to create radical change. The first edition of this index centers the ground work laid by Black and Indigenous communities of the past while highlighting the work these groups do in the present as they utilize their collective power and wisdom to continue the work towards solidarity and communal care. Let this index be proof of concept of what a gathering of ideas, organizations, reading materials and care could look like in one space.

LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

THIS RESOURCE WAS DEVELOPED ON UNCEDED LENNI LENAPE LAND.

The purpose of this index cannot be served without recognizing the trauma that has plagued a lot of our communities for centuries—and still effects us today. Since the unlawful settlement of these lands, the impacts of gentrification continue to displace members of our communities and deepen the effects of economic strife. In order to emphasize communal care and support for our neighbors, we must center the practices of communal care that were outlined by Indigenous communities of the past and present—while acknowledging the long-standing damage the impacts of colonization have had on the sovreign lands we currently inhabit.

BOROUGH-WIDE

The Moving Support Project is seeking Drivers

The Moving Support Project helps formerly unhoused people secure furniture to make their space into a real home. We coordinate with people who are willing to donate their items, and on move days a group of volunteers mobilizes to drive a 14-ft truck to pick up furniture, bring it to the new home and help organize the space. Interested volunteers must have a drivers license.

SUNSET PARK, BOROUGH PARK, BAY RIDGE

South Brooklyn Mutual Aid is seeking
Distribution Volunteers

South Brooklyn Mutual Aid is a mutual aid group situated in South Brooklyn. Every Saturday, SBKMA brings food to hundreds of families — this is the heart of what we do. You can volunteer at the warehouse at Brooklyn Army Terminal to help pack and load food boxes, or drive to deliver food to community members every Saturday between 1 - 3:30 p.m. Sign up by Fridays at 2 p.m. We’ll send you an email confirmation with instructions on Friday evening.
